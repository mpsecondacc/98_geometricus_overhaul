Terrestric photogrammetry and aerial photogrammetry

Aerial photogrammetry images are used to deliver high-resolution orthophotos and digital terrain models. For needs on a smaller scale, we have a high-precision drone DJI Phantom 4 RTK. For medium-range and corridor surveying purposes, we use Matrice M300 RTK in combination with L1/P1 sensor.

For needs on a larger scale we use Delta Quad Map VTOL and Sony A7RIV camera. The mentioned combination enables an autonomy of 2 hours and at the same time enables mapping of 1200 ha at a resolution of 3 cm/px. With the help of a Sony camera, we perform terrestrial and close-up photogrammetry.

LiDAR i terrestric laser scanning

Fusing together Applanix APX20 IMU and Riegl's miniVUX 3 laser scanner we deliver survey grade deliverables. One of many advantages over photogrammetry is the ability to filter out vegetation and classify ground. It is used in corridor mapping - roads, railways and transmission lines, complex industrial plants, forest inventory, large mining areas, ...

Multibeam hydrography

The WASSP S3PR Multibeam Echosounder (MBES) is purpose-built for geodetic mapping and surveying. By simultaneously generating up to 100 pings per second at an angle of 120 degrees and including advanced signal processing, we easily see a complete and accurate picture of the underwater bathymetry. This system is ideal for: detailed bathymetric survey, port survey and channel management, dredging, marine infrastructure inspection, wreck survey and search and rescue operations.
